Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is padel and how is it different from tennis?

Padel is a racquet sport that combines elements of tennis and squash. It's played on an enclosed court about one-third the size of a tennis court. The main differences are that the court has walls which are part of the game, the ball can be played after bouncing off them. The racquets are solid with no strings, and the scoring system is the same as tennis.

Do I need to bring my own equipment?

While you can bring your own padel racquet and balls, we also offer rental equipment for beginners or occasional players. Our pro shop stocks a wide range of padel racquets, balls, and accessories if you decide to purchase your own.

How do I book a court?

Courts can be booked through our website, mobile app, or by calling our reception. Members can book up to 7 days in advance, while non-members can book 3 days in advance. Bookings are made in 1-hour slots, but you can book multiple consecutive slots if available.

Do you offer coaching for beginners?

Yes! We offer introductory padel lessons for complete beginners, taught by our professional coaches. We also run regular beginner clinics where you can learn the basics in a group setting. These are great ways to start your padel journey and meet other players of a similar level.

Are there membership options available?

We offer several membership tiers to suit different needs, from full access plans to off-peak options. Members enjoy benefits like priority booking, discounted court rates, pro shop discounts, and access to member-only events and tournaments. Visit our reception or website for current membership options and rates.
